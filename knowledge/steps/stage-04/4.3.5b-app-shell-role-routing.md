## Playwright assertions proven
- Admin, lecturer, and student each logged in through real Supabase browser auth in separate browser contexts and landed on `/admin`, `/lecturer`, and `/student`.
- Role was confirmed through wrapper-backed `window.__xyzE2E.callMe()` and backend `/me`, not JWT claims.
- `refreshSession()` rotated the lecturer access token; the next wrapper-backed `/me` request used `Authorization: Bearer <new_access_token>`.
- A forced malformed bearer token produced a real backend 401; the wrapper signed out, redirected to `/login`, and left Supabase session null.
- Student navigation to `/admin` redirected to `/unauthorized`, not `/login`, with session preserved.
- Student `window.__xyzE2E.callAdminUsers()` returned a serializable failure with status 403, preserved session, and did not navigate to `/login`.
- With `NEXT_PUBLIC_TRACER_ENABLED=false`, `/tracer` returned 404/not-found and tracer controls did not render.
- AppShell logout cleared session, landed on `/login`, and a later `/lecturer` navigation redirected back to `/login`.

## Findings and fixes during verification
- Initial Playwright runs exposed timing around wrapper-triggered redirects: the test attempted to read `window.__xyzE2E` immediately after navigation to `/login`. The test was fixed to wait for hook registration after `/login` redirects. Assertions were not weakened.
- The rebuilt-container frontend type-check initially repeated stale TypeScript errors because the frontend container is image-based. Rebuilding the E2E stack made the container use the latest source; final type-check passed.
- A read-only reviewer found one enabled-tracer regression after Checkpoint D: wrapper `ForbiddenError` status/body were not shown by the tracer. `TracerClient` now preserves `status` and `body` from wrapper errors.

## Decisions made
No ADR required. The durable auth/routing model was already approved in the spec: role comes from backend `/me`, guards are client-side, and `/tracer` is retained but env-gated until deletion in 4.3.5e.
